People always ask: how do you make money doing this?

The honest answer is: many things, inconsistently, with a lot of anxiety.

But let me break it down properly, because I think the real picture is more useful than the inspirational highlight reel.

## The Six Streams (As Of Now)

### 1. Game Sales

This is the dream but it's also the most volatile. Revenue from actual games on itch.io and Steam. It spikes on launch, drops to near zero within two weeks, then occasionally ticks up during sales.

**Reality check:** Unless you make something that hits, game sales alone won't pay rent. This is a long game (no pun intended). I treat it as the creative core, not the financial foundation.

### 2. Patreon

My most stable income source. Patrons paying monthly for behind-the-scenes dev logs, early access, Discord roles, and the feeling of being part of something real.

The key insight: Patreon isn't about the perks. It's about belonging. People support creators they feel connected to. Consistency and honesty build that more than polished content ever will.

**What works:** Regular updates even when there's nothing exciting to report. Showing process, not just results.

### 3. Content (TikTok / YouTube / Blog)

Ad revenue is tiny. The value of content is everything else: community growth, SEO for the games, brand recognition, and a pipeline into all the other income streams.

Think of content as infrastructure, not income. You pay for it now (time and effort) and it pays you back later in compound interest.

### 4. Freelance / Contract Work

When cash flow is tight, freelance game dev work fills the gap. Level design, Godot scripting, game jam consulting. It's trading time for money, but it keeps the lights on while the creative projects breathe.

I cap this at 30% of my working time. More than that and the original work suffers.

### 5. Merchandise

Pending. But it's in the plan. Limited drops, physical things that people can hold. The goal is to make something I'd actually want to own.

### 6. Sponsored Content / Brand Deals

Occasional. I'm selective about this. If I wouldn't use the product, I don't promote it. The trust I've built with the audience is worth more than a one-off check.

## The Real Lesson

Diversify early. Don't wait until you "make it" on one stream before building others.

Also: track your money. I use a simple spreadsheet. Income sources, expenses, monthly net. Nothing fancy. Just visibility.

You can't manage what you can't see.
